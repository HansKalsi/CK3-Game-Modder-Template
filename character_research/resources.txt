Canute (I) (died November 12, 1035) was a Danish king of England (1016–35),
of Denmark (as Canute II; 1019–35), and of Norway (1028–35),
who was a power in the politics of Europe in the 11th century,
respected by both emperor and pope. Neither the place nor the date of his birth is known.
